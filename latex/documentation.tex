\begin{abstract}
Despite a significant push in the past decade to liberate and make data open
and accessible, the lack of any sensible means to query such data sets enmasse
makes their usefulness limited to those interested parties willing to incur the
cost of data integration. Today, a new wave of environmental data from IoT
devices is entering the scene; unlike that of previous generations of
environmental data which were static in time, this data now includes time and
geoloation information. Despite the large amounts of potentially useful data
that's becoming available, however, a platform on which enthusiast and
organizations alike can donate and liberate their data, and where data
scientists can take advantage of uniform querying in order to layer data from
different datasets in new and unexpected ways still remains an unrealized utopia.
With this project, we look at the current state of affairs of such static open
data platforms, inquire about why they're no longer serving their promise of
openness and failing in their usability (and therefore usefulness), and
introduce an early prototype for what such a system would look like if it
were built with cutting edge cloud technologies.
\end{abstract}
